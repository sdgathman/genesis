Shadowland: Beginnings: Week 60

Genesis 50

  • 50:3 "the Egyptians mourned for him 70 days" - this was the formal mourning period for a royal, as it was the time required for mummification.
  • 50:15 "perhaps Joseph will hate us" - not an unreasonable fear given human nature. "all the evil which we did to him" - they recognized their own sin, and feared justice.
  • 50:16 "before your father died, he commanded" - a rather unlikely tale. They didn't feel worthy to just ask for mercy.
  • 50:17 "Joseph wept" - that they were lying, and doubted his forgiveness.
  • 50:19 "Am I in the place of God?" - "Vengence is mine, saith the Lord" Romans 12:19
  • 50:20 "you meant it for evil" - Joseph does not romanticize the evil done. "God meant it for good" Romans 8:28
  • 50:24 "God will surely bring you up out of Egypt" Hebrews 11:22
  • 50:25 According to this and Hebrews 11:22, Joseph was not buried until they buried him in Schechem after the Exodus. Exodus 13:19, Joshua 24:32
  • 50:26 "a coffin in Egypt" - His coffin laid aboveground for the 100 or so years until it was taken back to Canaan. It was a silent witness for all those years that Israel was going back to the Promised Land, just as God said.
The generally accepted site of Joseph's tomb is on the West Bank in Nablus. It was handed over to the Palestinian Authority as part of the Oslo Accords, with the requirement of free access to people of faith. Presumably, the grave would contain an Egyptian mummy - but violence between Israelis and Palestinians has prevented access.
