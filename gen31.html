Shadowland: Beginnings: Week 31

משחק בינגו

Isaac the Man

In this chapter, we see Isaac encounter tests eirily similar to what Abraham encountered, doing better on some, worse on others. His personality is contrasted with Abraham's with just a few masterful strokes of the stylus. These scenes set the stage for the sad events of the next chapter.
  1. 26:1 Having done better than his father on the first test, we move on to Isaac's second test.
  2. 26:2 Unlike Abraham, Isaac never left Caanan. In the same way, Jesus will never leave Caanan - until He returns to His Father and then comes again to judge the living and the dead.
  3. 26:5 Ultimately, Isaac fails to follow God's instruction when he attempts to make Esau the child of Promise. Isaac has free will, but God's purpose will be accomplished.
  4. 26:6 Isaac likes living in civilization, instead of a tent.
  5. 26:7 But just like with Abraham, the worldly civilization erodes Isaac's trust in God's promises.
  6. 26:8 And just like with Abraham, Isaac is found out. I wonder if this Abimelech (a title meaning "king father") "just happened" to be looking out a window, or if he knew what happened with Abraham and was suspicious.
  7. 26:9 At least Isaac tells the truth, like Abraham did the second time.
  8. 26:16 Whereas the former Abimelech saw God's blessing on Abraham and wanted to keep him around, this Abimelech sees God's blessing on Isaac and feels threatened by it.
  9. 26:20-22 Unlike Abraham, Isaac seeks to avoid conflict.
  10. 26:28 Now that Isaac is finally separated from the world, this Abimelech, like his predecessor, sees the light.
  11. 26:33 We now have several wells and a city named Beersheba.
  12. 26:34 Esau again demonstates his disdain for God's promises and covenant - making the Isaac's disobedience next time all the more egregious.
  13. 26:35 They were vexed in their righteous souls, but only Rebecca remembers God's choice of the heir - and like Sarah, she takes matters into her own hands.
